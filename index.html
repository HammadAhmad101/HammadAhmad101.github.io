INT. THE WORK

Selected work

Entry 01

Footage-grounded scripting for five faceless channels

The brief
Paranormal compilation channels live or die on credibility. Scripts written without watching the footage drift into generic filler — and viewers notice when the narration doesn't match the screen.
The work
I built a video-analysis workflow with Claude that extracts frames from source footage, so every entry I write describes what's actually visible — then layered each channel's house style on top: entry length, tone, rotating ending types, banned-word lists.
The result
Consistent, on-brand scripts across five channels — including Paranormal Road Rider, Uncharted Travel, and The Abandoned Camper — with narration viewers can trust against the footage.
Entry 02

High-volume production without the drift

The brief
Compilation videos need 4,500–5,000-word scripts — dozens of self-contained entries per video — delivered fast, without the tone flattening into copy-paste sameness by entry twenty.
The work
I write in disciplined batches of 30–45 entries, each 90–100 words with its own hook, unique opening, and one of five rotating ending types — never two of the same kind in a row.
The result
Full-length scripts on schedule, entry after entry, with pacing and variety engineered in rather than left to chance.
Entry 03

A retention framework for underperforming scripts

The brief
Some scripts looked fine on the page but lost viewers mid-video. The channel needed to know why — structurally, not anecdotally.
The work
I diagnosed the structural weak points and built a repeatable framework: intensity peaks, entry sequencing, re-engagement line rotation, and ending-type variety — then wrote it up as a standard operating procedure the whole channel can follow.
The result
A house playbook that turns retention from guesswork into checklist — applied to every script since.
Entry 04

SEO copy that clients could measure

The brief
UK-based clients, including Twicsy UK, needed website copy and blog content that ranked — not just filled pages.
The work
At Media Foundation 360 I ran keyword research, wrote SEO-optimized copy, and briefed leadership directly on content direction and progress.
The result
Seven months of shipped, search-driven content for international clients — and the research habits I still bring to every script.

INT. ABOUT

About

I've always been drawn to the stories that sit just outside the streetlight — the dashcam clip nobody can explain, the hallway that shouldn't have footsteps in it. Writing for faceless YouTube channels lets me take that fascination seriously: my job is to look at strange footage carefully and tell you what happened without breaking the spell.

Off the clock, I read Urdu classical literature and philosophy of mind — which sounds unrelated until you've tried to narrate a ghost story with restraint. I graduated from the University of the Punjab in 2025 (BSc Digital Media & Communication, 3.80 CGPA), and I build my own AI workflows — Claude, APIs, automation — so the craft scales without the writing going stale.
